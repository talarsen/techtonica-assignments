  • What are semantic and non-semantic elements in HTML?

    • Answer: Semantic elements provide for accessibilty, SEO, and are easy to understand. Semantic elements provide information about the content between the opening and closing tags. Examples include

    • <header>
    • <footer>
    • <aside>
    • <article>

    Answer: Non-semantic elements do not convey meaning. Examples include

    • <div>
    • <span>

  • How do you write comments in HTML?

    • Answer: Begins with a <! – – and the comment closes with – –>

  • Why is indentation used in HTML?

    • Answer: Indentation is very important in HTML because it helps the readability of the code. Spacing and indentation should be consistent throughout your code. Many developers choose to use 4-space or 2-space indentation. In HTML, each nested tag should be indented exactly once inside of its parent tag. It is important to be consistent in whichever spacing you choose.

  • What does the line 'meta charset=“utf-8”' mean?

    • Answer: Meta charset means you are telling your browser to use the UTF-8 character encoding, which is a method of converting your typed characters into machine-readable code
